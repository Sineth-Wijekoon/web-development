INTRODUCTION TO GLOBAL HUNGER

Global Hunger is one of the most pressing problems of the modern day. Many communities are still struggling with food insecurity, malnutrition and starvation. It is also connection with poverty. This crisis is a must to be addressed and resolved and to make the oppurtunity for everyone to have access to nutritious food and the oppurtunity to thrive.

CAUSES OF GLOBAL HUNGER

  • Poverty - Low income families cannot afford food
  • Conflict and war - Agricultural Systems destroyed and Food items scarce
  • Climate Change - Events like floods or droughts can destroy agricultural lands massively
  • Economic Instability - Inflation, and the countries economy makes food innaccessible to certain groups of people

Rates of Global Hunger

  • 735 Million People worldwide suffer from Hunger
  • 45 Million Children suffer from malnutrition
  • 1.3 Billion tons of food is wasted, enough to feed the hunger
  • Africa and Asia have the highest rates of hunger

EFFECTS OF GLOBAL HUNGER

  • Health Consequences - malnutrition can increase health vulnerabilities
  • Child Development Issues - Hunger can lead to stunted growth and lower academic performance
  • Economic Impact - Hunger leads causes lower productivity thus leading to lower economic growth
  • Social Instability - Hunger can lead to conflict over resources and political Instability

SOLUTIONS TO GLOBAL HUNGER

  • Improving agricultural practices - Sustainable Farming techniques
  • Reducing Food waste - Better consumption habits and storage facilities can decrease food waste
  • Economic Support - Investing in farmers and their wages can increase the farming productivity
  • Access to Education - Education communities about nutrition and food sustainability can empower them
